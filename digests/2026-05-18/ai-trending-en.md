## Hot List Observations

Today’s distribution is strikingly **agent‑centric**: 10 of the 12 AI‑related trending repos fall under **AI Agents / Workflows** or **AI Applications** that directly support agents. Notable is the emergence of **agent skill registries** — both `tech-leads-club/agent-skills` and `K-Dense-AI/scientific-agent-skills` — which provide curated, validated skills for multiple coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, etc.). This signals a shift toward **standardised, shareable skill ecosystems** rather than monolithic agents.

Another clear pattern is **local/private AI**: `openhuman`, `DreamServer`, `open-webui`, and `codegraph` all emphasise offline operation, privacy, and no cloud dependencies. The **code knowledge graph** approach (`codegraph`) is a novel project type that aims to reduce token consumption and tool calls for AI coding agents by pre‑indexing codebases.

There is no direct evidence of a specific LLM release driving today’s activity; rather, the interest appears organic, driven by the maturation of agent toolchains and the desire for customisable, private AI workflows.
